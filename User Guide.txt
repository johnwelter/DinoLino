USER GUIDE
------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
GLOBAL METHODS:

Undo:	
Undoes the last action performed by the user. This returns the metadata of the previous calculation and removes the last drawn operation.

Redo:	
Redoes the last action that was undone by the user. This returns the metadata of the next calculation and redraws the operation.

Clear:	
Clears all drawn operations and resets the metadata to its initial state.

CURVATURE MODE: 

Geometric calculations to calculate curvature 

3-Point Arc: 

Approximate a curve as the arc of a circle using three points. 
Click twice to draw a line from each end of the curve (your 'chord'), then click a third time in the midpoint of the curve to draw the chord bisector (aka sagitta). 
Click a fourth time to begin another curvature calculation.

Central Angle: A measure of curvature. The central angle of a circular arc drawn using three points. 
Aspect ratio: Length of the first line drawn (chord) divided by length of the second line (sagitta).

n-Point Spline:

Click any amount of times to create a spline curve. Double click on the final point to finish the curve and return metadata. Click a single time to begin another spline.

Turning Angle: A measure of curvature. Change in angle of the tangent lines at the start and end points of the curve divided by the length of the curve.
Chord-Arc Ratio: A measure of curvature. The length of the straight line (chord) connecting the start and end points of the curve divided by the length of the curve.
------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
TRIANGLE MODE:

Draws a triangle on the image and returns its three interior angles and proportions. 

Click three times anywhere on the image to create a triangle. Click a fourth time to begin drawing another triangle.

Angle A:		The angle at vertex A.
Angle B:		The angle at vertex B.
Angle C:		The angle at vertex C.
Aspect Ratio:	Length of the longest side of the triangle divided by its height.
Relative Size:	Area of the current triangle divided by the area of the previous triangle.

Note: Triangle height is the perpendicular distance from its longest side to the opposite vertex.
------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
DRAW MODE:

Draw basic shapes and lines on the image and return their relative proportions.

For shapes, click once to begin drawing, drag to determine size and shape, and click a second time to finish.
For lines, click twice anywhere on the image to draw a straight line between two points.
For angles, type the desired angle in the text box. Click twice anywhere on the image draw line AB. Line BC will be constrained to the specified angle. Click a third time to finish.

Aspect Ratio: Width of the drawn shape divided by its height. NA for lines and angles.
Relative Size: Area of the drawn shape divided by the area of the previous shape. NA for lines and angles.
Line Ratio: Length of the drawn line divided by the length of the previous line. NA for shapes.
